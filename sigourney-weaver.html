Who is Sigourney Weaver?

Susan Alexandra Weaver a.k.a Sigourney Weaver is an American actress and film producer. She had a little part in her 1977 film debut, but she quickly created a name for herself in Hollywood.

The actress received her first starring role as “Ellen Ripley” in the well-known film “Alien” in barely two years.

Weaver’s portrayal of an action heroine was well-received in the Alien film series. She returned to the part of “Ripley.”

Weaver received a nomination for the “Academy Award” for “Best Actress” for her outstanding performance in just a short span of her acting journey.

Early Life of Sigourney Weaver

Sigourney Weaver was born on 8th October 1949 in New York City, United States, and holds American nationality.

Elizabeth Inglis, her mother, was a Colchester, England, native and an English actress.

Sylvester “Pat” Weaver Jr., Weaver’s father, was a Los Angeles-born American television executive who ran NBC from 1953 to 1955 and founded the Today Show in 1952.

Winstead “Doodles” Weaver, Pat’s brother, was a comic and Mad contributor.

The American lineage of her father’s family included Dutch, English, Scots-Irish, and Scottish ancestry.

Academics & Passion For Acting

Weaver adopted the moniker “Sigourney” at the age of 14, borrowing it from a supporting role in The Great Gatsby.

Before enrolling at the Ethel Walker School (Walker’s) in Simsbury, Connecticut.

She temporarily attended the Brearley School and Chapin School in New York, where she first became interested in performance art.

She performed a character inspired by Rudolph Valentino in an adaption of “The Sheik”, and one of her earliest parts was in a school production of the poem “The Highwayman.”

During one summer in Southbury, Connecticut, she also participated in the theatre productions of “A Streetcar Named Desire” and “You Can’t Take It With You”.

By the time she was 11, Weaver reportedly stood at 5 ft 10+12 in (179 cm), which had a significant effect on her self-esteem.

She enrolled in Sarah Lawrence College in 1967. She switched to Stanford University as an English major after completing her first year.

Weaver was quite active in theatre at Stanford. In 1974, she received her Master of Fine Arts from Yale.

Sigourney Weaver Career

In 1977, Sigourney Weaver made her cinematic debut in the Woody Allen picture “Annie Hall.

In the film, she starred opposite Allen in a supporting role. Despite having a brief role, she caught people’s attention.

She was cast as a warrant officer dubbed “Ellen Ripley” in the popular film “Alien” in 1979. The film was a great smash and Sigourney returned for three of the sequels.

Along with the “Alien” series, she also starred alongside Mel Gibson in “The Year of Living Dangerously.” She appeared in both the original Ghostbusters and the sequel.

Hard work Pays Off

She appeared in movies like “Gorillas in the Mist” and “Working Girl” in 1988. Weaver received “Golden Globe Awards” for Best Actress and Best Supporting.

She was also a finalist for two Academy Awards: “Best Actress” for “Gorillas in the Mist” and “Best Supporting Actress” for “Working Girl.

Weaver acted in “Alien 3” in 1992. She portrayed “Queen Isabella” in the movie “1492: Conquest of Paradise” that same year.

Weaver made numerous film appearances in the 1990s, including “Jeffrey” and “The Ice Storm.” She was nominated for a “Golden Globe” and won a “BAFTA Award” for the latter.

She portrayed “Mary Griffith” in her debut TV film, “Prayers for Bobby,” which was released in 2009.

The actress was nominated for an “Emmy Award,” “Golden Globe Award,” and Screen Actors Guild Award” for this portrayal, which got positive reviews.

Weaver collaborated with director James Cameron that same year to star in the sweeping science fiction film “Avatar.”

She portrayed “Vicky Zelda” in the comedy “Crazy on the Outside” the following year.

Weaver also appeared as “Ramona Clark” in the comedy “You Again.” She received praise for her efforts in science fiction movies at the 2010 “Scream Awards.”

She has acted in several plays in addition to movies, such as the 1980 Off-Broadway production.

Sigourney Weaver’s Personal Life

On 1st October 1984, Weaver married Jim Simpson who is a stage director. They have a daughter named Charlotte and reside in Manhattan.

Weaver started supporting the Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund after directing Gorillas in the Mist and is currently its honorary chairwoman.

She is recognized as an environmentalist and received an award from the Explorers Club for her efforts.
