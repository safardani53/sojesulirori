Coca-Cola quietly discontinues popular flavor as shoppers say 'you can still make it at home'

April 2024 Â· 2 minute read

COCA-Cola fans have mourned the end of a fan-favorite soft drink, but they may have found an at-home solution.

In an X post (formerly known as Twitter), Coca-Cola confirmed the flavor was discontinued.

A customer reached out to the brand asking if the Cinnamon Coke had been discontinued from stores.

The company responded with the confirmation.

"Unfortunately, Cinnamon Coke was a limited-edition flavor and was only available while supplies lasted," the brand confirmed.

"Thanks for being a flavor fan and we'll let the team know you'd like to see it back again."

The fan said that customers who liked the limited edition drink can still "make it at home."

However, fans are still mourning the loss of the grocery store item.

"I'm still forever missing Cinnamon Coca-Cola," another fan said on X.

HOME MADE COLA

The Coca-Cola can be made with four ingredients, food blogs claimed.

The Cinnamon-flavored soda can be made with cinnamon bark syrup, a lime wedge, ice and Coca-Cola, according to Kerry Food Service.

Customers can combine 12-ounced of the soda with a half ounce of the bark syrup to replicate the taste, the recipe said.

COCA-GONE

Coca-Cola has taken to X to confirm several other fan-favorite flavors are fully discontinued.

In August, the company confirmed that it axed the Orange Vanilla Coke.

The company said the drink combined vanilla with a hint of orange into the classic crisp Coca-Cola finish.

Customers begged for the brand to bring back the flavor on X.

"The other weird flavors you're coming up with are gag me," one customer claimed.

Coca-Cola also stated it discontinued the zero-sugar version as well.

The company also discontinued some other non-name brand drinks.

Coca-Cola discontinued the Strawberry Cucumber Aha sparkling water - customers have expressed dismay at the decision.

"I need this in my life again," one customer cried.

"It's all I can think about."
