Five Unknown Facts About Disneys Latest Hit Encanto

February 2024 · 4 minute read

Encanto, Disney’s most recent success, is well-known for its representation of true Colombian history as well as its outstanding melodies. Along with the heartwarming tale, the excellent animation and scenery contributed to the pleasure. The representation of Latin American culture by Disney in its animation makes the film as engaging as it possibly be. The entertainment company’s specialty has always been inclusivity. It has brought many cultures together on the same platform by making cartoons from many ethnicities throughout the globe.

For almost a half-century, the Madrigal family has utilized their magical abilities to benefit the people of their community. The entire foundation of their family is under attack, and they must band together to safeguard their abilities. When all hope seemed to be gone, they rediscover magic by mending their damaged family. Five little-known facts about Disney’s recent smash Encanto

The Disney film highlights Latin American culture via cartoons that include a diversity of colors and topics. It introduces viewers from all around the globe to a certain culture. With a large audience, the film will familiarize people with Latin American culture and provide them the opportunity to comprehend it. Mirabel’s bright depiction has viewers applauding with excitement as she takes them on her family’s trip.

2.) The plant inside the boot in Bruno’s lair looks a lot like the one in WALL-E.

According to Parents Magazine,

“The plant from WALL.E. is tucked away in Bruno’s den. The brown footwear is sitting on a shelf in his room, next to a solitary green sprout.”

The very recognizable device in Bruno’s lair seems to be a supplemental feature included by Disney to lend more flare to the film. It is a Disney tradition to leave hidden hints that may lead to subsequent Disney films or just to make the setting more appealing.

3.) The topic you agreed not to discuss becomes a huge subject of rumor.

Though the Madrigal family declined to discuss the runaway Bruno, he was a frequent subject of discussion around the community. They even wrote a song about not bringing up Bruno. According to reports, the song We Don’t Talk About Bruno was inspired by familial circumstances in which the thing that isn’t intended to be spoken is also the thing that is most talked about.

4.) Mirabel’s rendition of the Family Madrigal is the quickest song ever performed by a Disney character.

Mirabel reveals her whole family to the audience as well as the rest of the hamlet. Stephanie Beatrize managed to get the vocals right despite the fact that it got tough to follow at one point.

According to Buzzfeed,

“In Disney history, Mirabel’s singing in The Family Madrigal is the quickest singing a character has had to accomplish.”

Mirabel sang as quickly as she could, providing a beautiful and entertaining opening to the narrative. She described her whole family’s abilities and the magical Casita.

5.) Stephanie Beatriz portrays two parts that are diametrically opposed: Rosa Diaz in Brooklyn Nine-Nine and Mirabel Madrigal in Encanto.

Rosa Diaz is nothing like Mirabel Madrigal, yet Stephanie Betrize managed to play Mirabel with aplomb. According to Comic Book Resources,

“Stephanie Beatrize is the protagonist’s voice, and she couldn’t be more different from her notorious portrayal as Detective Rosa Diaz in Brooklyn Nine-Nine.”

Mirabel’s persona is full of energy and excitement, and he is always in the desire to sing and dance. Rosa Diaz, on the other hand, exhibits no emotion and is a terrifying figure who enjoys beating people up. She must be quite gifted to have played such disparate parts. Encanto was released in November 2021 as The Walt Disney Company’s 60th film.
