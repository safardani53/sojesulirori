Meet Eli Manning Parents: Family And Relationship Explore

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Fans are curious about Eli Manning’s parents. They are curious about his personal life. Elisha Nelson Manning is a former quarterback in the National Football League. He spent 16 seasons in the National Football League with the New York Giants (NFL). Eli is the youngest son of Archie Manning, the famed quarterback, and a member of the Manning football dynasty. He is also the younger brother of Peyton Manning. Manning got the Maxwell and Johnny Unitas Golden Arm Awards in his last season at Ole Miss, where he played college football. The San Diego Chargers selected him first overall in the 2004 NFL Draft, and he was eventually traded to the Giants.

Manning, Eli Archie Manning, and Olivia Williams Manning are his parents.

He is the third child of NFL quarterback Elisha Archibald “Archie” Manning III and Olivia (née Williams), both of whom are from Mississippi. Peyton and Cooper, his elder brothers, were football players when they were younger. Manning represented the Greenies in football and basketball while attending Isidore Newman School in New Orleans. In high school, Eli Manning threw for 7,389 yards and 89 touchdowns. He chose to play collegiate football at the University of Mississippi on February 7, 1999. Manning came at Ole Miss under the shadow of his brother, Indianapolis Colts quarterback Peyton, and his father, an Ole Miss “folk hero.”

While playing with the Rebels, Manning established or equaled 45 single-game, season, and career records. His career stats include 10,119 passing yards, a passer rating of 137.7, and 81 touchdown passes, placing him third on the SEC lifetime record (tied for sixth on the SEC career list).

Eli Manning’s Children and Family

He proposed to Abby McGrew of Nashville, Tennessee, in 2007. Manning and McGrew have been dating since they were the University of Mississippi, undergraduates. McGrew studied fashion in New York. On April 19, 2008, the pair married in a private ceremony in San José del Cabo, Mexico. Manning and Abby had four children, three girls, and one boy. Their headquarters are in Summit, New Jersey. After Hurricane Katrina, Eli and Peyton Manning volunteered.

The Mannings helped send 30,000 pounds (14,000 kg) of goods to the citizens of New Orleans, including water, Gatorade, infant formula, pumice, and pillows. In 2007, he began a five-year fundraising campaign to gather $2.5 million towards the establishment of “The Eli Manning Children’s Clinics” at Blair E. Batson Hospital. This is for the University of Mississippi Medical Center’s Children.

“I am humbled by their efforts and am proud to make this five-year commitment to help raise money for the development of this cutting-edge clinic that will benefit Mississippi families for years to come,” Manning said in 2007. Archie Manning, his father, has also contributed financially to the Katrina relief effort.
