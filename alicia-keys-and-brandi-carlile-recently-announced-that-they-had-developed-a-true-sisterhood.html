Alicia Keys And Brandi Carlile Recently Announced That They Had Developed A True Sisterhood

September 2024 · 3 minute read

Alicia Keys and Brandi Carlile have shared a real kinship with one another. Carlile discussed her relationship with Keys on The Ellen DeGeneres Show on April 1 and said:

“I was telling everyone earlier that we’ve just been getting to know each other for a few years, but we’ve grown to kind of become sisters.”

Alicia responded with:

“With you, Brandi, I feel like I’ve found someone who is really a sister. I can phone you and say ‘Hey,’ and you can call me and say ‘Hey,’ and we’ll both show there. It’s a pretty unusual occurrence, therefore I treasure it.”

Alicia and Brandi also discussed their Grammy-nominated duet, A Beautiful Noise, which will be published in 2020. The song has been nominated for “Song of the Year” at the Grammy Awards, which will take place on April 3, 2022. On the afternoon chat program, the vocalists even played an acoustic version of the song.

Carlile added that Keys had influenced her children, giving her a home video of her daughters Evangeline and Elijah performing the 2012 song Girl on Fire. Carlile later expressed gratitude to Keys for the song’s uplifting tone, saying,

“I simply wanted to show you because I wanted to tell you how pleasant it is to hear words like those coming out of the lips of my children.” It means a lot to me, and thank you for sending such an encouraging letter.”

Everything you need to know about Brandi Carlile

Brandi Carlile, born on June 1, 1981, has recorded seven studio albums and 18 Grammy Awards nominations since 2021. The singer received the most nominations for a female artist at the 61st Annual Grammy Awards. In 2019, the 40-year-old created the ‘Highwomen,’ an all-female quartet with Amanda Shires, Maren Morris, and Natalie Hemby. Their first album, The High women, was published the following year, and their song Crowded Table received the Grammy Award for Best Country Table Song in 2021.

The Ravensdale, Washington native dropped out of high school to pursue a music career and study the piano and guitar. Brandi Carlile, her first album, was released in 2005. It received favorable reviews but had minimal financial success. Carlile rose to prominence with her 2007 song The Story, which was certified Gold by the RIAA in 2017 for sales of 50,000 copies. She then published Cover Stories, which had 14 musicians covering songs from her first album, The Story.

Brandi’s fifth album, The Firewatcher’s Daughter, received her first Grammy nomination for Best Americana Album in 2016 and peaked at number nine on the Billboard 200. Her sixth studio album, By the Way, I Forgive You, was released in 2018 and garnered positive reviews from critics.

In These Silent Days, the songwriter’s sixth album was both critically and financially successful. It debuted at No. 11 on the Billboard 200, as well as No. 1 on the Top Rock Albums and Folk Albums lists. Brandi has also participated in several activism projects and served as an advocate for topics such as raising awareness about health concerns, LGBT rights, and women’s empowerment.
