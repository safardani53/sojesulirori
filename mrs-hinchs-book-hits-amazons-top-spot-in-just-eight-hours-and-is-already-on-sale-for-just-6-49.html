Mrs Hinchs book hits Amazons top spot in just eight hours - and is already on sale for just

April 2024 · 3 minute read

WITH any cleaning product she uses becoming a sell-out out nationwide; it’s fair to say that Mrs Hinch has some selling power.

And her fans are rejoicing as her bestselling book is now available for half the recommended retail price.

Hinch Yourself Happy: All The Best Cleaning Tips to Shine Your Sink and Soothe Your Soul became topped Amazon’s best sellers list within just eight hours of its release.

Despite its unprecedented popularity the online retailer has already slashed the price of the cleaning guru’s first tome.

With an RRP of £12.99 Amazon is offering it to customers for just £6.49, saving them a total of £6.50.

And the bargain hasn’t gone unnoticed by Hinch fans with one sharing their purchase on the Facebook group Extreme Couponing and Bargains UK.

Posting a snap of the newly arrived book she wrote: “Look what arrived today #Hinching #MrsHinch #Hinched Available on Amazon for £6.49”

It seems that fellow Hinchers were equally eager to get their hands on the must-read with the photo receiving from hundreds of comments.

The book was released on Wednesday at midnight where it headed straight to the top of the best sellers list.

Mrs Hinch, aka Sophie Hinchliffe, 29, from Essex thanked her fans for their support in her Instagram story writing “#1 Hinchers! Actually number 1! You’re all unbelievable! Thank you from my heart.”

The expectant mum is also set to do her first book signing today as she told Chris Evans on his Virgin Radio Show this morning.

On the show today she was quizzed on her cleaning tips by loyal fans who were desperate to know her secrets.

Speaking to Chris, Mrs Hinch said: "Thank you so much for having me I've loved it. I've never even been to a book signing before so I'm just going to wing it. As long as my hinchers are there I'll be alright."

Talking about her future plans, she said: "Baby Hinch is next on the agenda so it'll be about being home and loving that."

When pressed on whether she would end up hiring a cleaner after her career success, Chris said: "You're going to have to because you'll be so busy. Never say never."
