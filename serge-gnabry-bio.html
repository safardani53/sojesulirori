Serge Gnabry | Early Life & Career | What Nationality Is Serge Gnabry?

Serge David Gnabry was born on July 14, 1995, in Stuttgart, Germany, to Jean-Hermann Gnabry and Birgit Gnabry.

Moreover, his father hails from Ivory Coast and is an African man, whereas his mother is a native of Germany.

Hence, it is clear that the German winger belongs to a mixed race. Jean decided to travel to Germany to learn the language, return home, and teach the same.

However, while he was in Germany, he fell in love with Birgit, and Jean’s stay became permanent.

With Serge’s birth, Hermann devoted his life to guiding his son toward his goals by becoming his advisor. Initially, Gnabry had no passion for football whatsoever.

The Stuttgart native, as a child, watched his idol, Usain Bolt, who is a retired Jamaican sprinter.

As a result, he was more into track and field, and football was secondary.

On the other hand, as Usain set records upon records, Serge felt it would be difficult to catch up to his idol, thereby abandoning his plans to be a sprinter one day.

Eventually, the German found his calling for football.

Without a moment of delay, the young aspirant joined his local team, TSV Weissach. Although the local team was only a harboring ground, it opened up passages to other bigger clubs.

Serge Gnabry | Career: Club & Country | How Many Goals Has Gnabry Scored This Season?

Starting with local clubs from smaller divisions to Arsenal’s snatched from Stuttgart, Gnabry’s future looked bright as a sunny day.

Having looked up to Mesut Ozil since his childhood, it was a matter of time before he got to play with him at Arsenal.

Firstly, he played with the U-18 squad upon joining and was promoted to the reserves after the manager saw promising talents in the young boy.

Immediately in the 2012/13 season, the German winger debuted for Arsenal in a preseason game against FC Koln.

Likewise, in a League Cup game against Coventry City, Serge donned the Gunner jersey in a competitive match, replacing Oxlade-Chamberlain on September 26, 2012.

Again, he started in a defeat against Norwich City in his first-ever Premier League game.

Despite the loss, it was a proud moment for Gnabry, and he also became the youngest-ever debutante, 17 years and 98 days, second only to Jack Wilshere.

In like manner, he made his Champions League debut against his home country club Schalke 04 in a 2-0 loss.

Furthermore, with Arsenal, a lot needed to be achieved for the budding star. In a competition called the NextGen Series, Serge netted the only goal to seal the victory against CSKA Moscow U19s.

In the same way, a goal against Chelsea U19s and Liverpool U21s was added to his scoresheet.

Moving on, in the 2013/14 season, the German forward sent out waves through the Premier League with a praiseworthy start to a new season.

Eventually, he received a nomination for Golden Boy Award and a new five-year deal with Arsenal.

However, success stories are not devoid of failures, as an injury-plagued 2014/15 season saw him relegated to the bench and, in August 2015, a move to West Bromwich.

Back To Bundesliga

After a failed experience with the Baggies, it was time for Gnabry to return to Arsenal.

Again, there was nothing left for him at Arsenal either, so Bundesliga side Werder Bremen signed him in the July transfer window of 2016.

Nevertheless, it comes without a doubt that the then-manager of Arsenal, Arsene Wenger was incredibly fond of the young German.

Even today, Serge is grateful for the experiences he had under the legendary manager.

Serge Gnabry as Werder Bremen player

Next, Germany was the country where he would genuinely get a chance to shine.

Werder Bremen, Gnabry opened his scoresheet with a goal against Borussia Monchengladbach and went on to tally 11 goals in 27 league appearances with the Bundesliga club.

Like many things, a superb performance seldom fails to catch the eye, and in this case, Bayern Munich was monitoring the young winger’s development.

In other words, he would represent the record champions on June 11, 2017.

While this may be true, Gnabry knew to make it at Bayern, he would need enough playtime, and for this reason, he asked the board for a loan move.

Three days later, the winger traveled to Hoffenheim on a season-long loan.

One of the biggest memories etched in Bundesliga’s fans was his long-range screamer against RB Leipzig that ended in a 4-0 victory.

Similarly, Serge’s efforts landed Hoffenheim a Champions League spot in the upcoming season.

Becoming A Bavarian

Now, it was time for the German international to cement his place with the Bavarian club. Since the legendary Franck Ribery and Arjen Robben were departing at the end of the 2018/19 season, all faith rested upon Serge and his winger partner Kingsley Coman.

On September 1, 2018, the Ivorian-German forward made an official debut for the Bundesliga giants to victory over VfB Stuttgart.

After scoring his first goal against SC Freiburg, Gnabry would register ten goals in 30 league appearances.

Once upon a time, Tony Pulis, West Brom’s manager, stated,

Serge has come here to play games but he just hasn’t been for me, at the moment, at that level to play the games.”

Four years later, he would receive a huge slap in the face when Gnabry shot past Tottenham in a Champions League game, a total of four times.

Oh, how the tables turned, and maybe Pulis regrets his statements or even lets Gnabry go.

Nevertheless, it was only the start of what the skillful winger would demonstrate in the 2019/20 campaign.

In a 7-2 thrashing of Premier League club, Tottenham Hotspurs, Serge scored a poker, rightfully earning MOTM in the CL group stage game.

Sometime later, in the Champions League’s knockout stage, the Bayern winger scored a brace in a 3-0 defeat of Chelsea.

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the UCL games have been indefinitely postponed, and hence the second-leg games were not played.

International Career

Notwithstanding his club successes, the uberly talented winger represented his birth country on the international stage, primarily in the youth levels like the U-16s, U-17s, and U-18s.

Afterward, with the U-21 squad, Gnabry celebrated the European U-21 Championship victory, which paved the way for his nomination in the 2016 Summer Olympics squad. Again, the winger didn’t fail to impress.

Gnabry scored a goal against Mexico in a 2-2 draw, two against South Korea for the equalizer, and another 2 in a 10-0 battering of Fiji.

Likewise, a single goal against Portugal helped Germany advance to the semi-finals and, in the end, clinched the silver medal.

Yet, the main chapter of his life had to be unearthed.

After receiving a call-up to the senior team, Serge secured his place in the starting lineup after scoring a hat-trick against San Marino in the 2018 World Cup Qualifier.

In the end, things didn’t look good for Germany, as the Bayern winger sustained an injury, and the national side would go crashing out of the tournament in the group stage.

All hope is not lost for Germany as the entire roster got revitalized with additions of young and vibrant players like Leroy Sane, Serge Gnabry, Timo Werner, Niklas Sule, etc.

Further, Serge, in 2019, broke Miroslav Klose’s record of scoring ten goals in 13 matches to the former’s 11 games. The Bayern forward is tied with Gerd Muller’s record of 13 goals in 13 matches.

Serge Gnabry | Net Worth & Transfers History

Net Worth

In particular, the German international garnered a net worth of a whopping €8 million as of 2021. In Munich, he earns €3 million ($3.11 m) annually, and it doesn’t go without saying that playing for one of the biggest clubs has its perks.

Early Transfer

According to the transfer market, VfB Stuttgart sold Gnabry for €100 k ($103789) to Premier League Club Arsenal.

Similarly, the top-flight side loaned out the winger to West Bromwich Albion, and a year later, the Gunners sold one of their prospects to Werder Bremen for a reported €5 million ($5.19 m) in 2016.

At the same time, the German international recorded a market value of €2.5 million ($2.6 m).

A year later, Bundesliga record champions swapped in to acquire the services and handed in €8 million sums to Werder.

Concurrently, the up-and-coming winger was already lighting the Bundesliga on fire, and with that, his market value spiked to €15 million ($14.97 m) in 2017.

Thereafter, a loan spell at TSG Hoffenheim saw him retain that value, and on June 30, 2018, Serge would join the ranks of Bayern Munich permanently.

Further, it is astounding to report that from a market value of €500 K at Arsenal, Gnabry recorded €20 million in his first year as a player for Bayern.

Recent Transfer

Gradually, the value caught an upward trend, and on March 11, 2020, the sensational winger topped his career-high market value of €90 million ($93.42).

However, this value was only momentary due to the economic crisis waged by COVID-19 during that month.

Thus, the value dropped to $74.88 million, and even then, he ranks 3rd just behind Kai Havertz and Jadon Sancho and 5th among all right-wingers in the world.

Well, the highest value or not, Serge definitely enjoys his time at Bayern. After signing the contract with the Bavarians, the German international receives a spectacular $5.20 million yearly.

The winger is also associated with Common Goal, a pledge-based organization that uses football to bring about social change and help improve the lives of the public.

Similarly, his external income stems from his endorsement deal with the sporting brand Adidas.

The company has the responsibility to outfit Serge. Contrarily, during the off-season, Gnabry travels to Dubai, LA, etc., to spend his holidays.

He has his contract extended till 2026 in 2022.
